# Separate safety allegations from confirmed findings

A Safety Report remains private and does not affect a member's Trust Record until Jualokal reviews and confirms it. Reports use structured categories, require a written description, permit optional evidence, immediately block contact between the parties, and do not disclose the reporter's identity to the reported member. Jualokal may temporarily pause transactions when a report indicates credible immediate physical danger, but only a confirmed finding changes trust status or produces a lasting suspension. This balances rapid protection with fairness to a person who has merely been accused.

A member affected by a confirmed finding receives its category and a redacted explanation and may submit one Safety Appeal within seven days. A different reviewer considers the appeal, physical-safety restrictions remain active during review, the reporter's identity stays protected, and the member receives a final written outcome. The prototype simulates this outcome without requiring a moderator dashboard.

The report, appeal, and suspension details above are provisional prototype safety policy. Their exact categories, deadlines, thresholds, evidence standards, and consequences must be reconsidered with qualified safety and legal input before a real launch.
