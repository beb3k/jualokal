## Judging alignment

The four judging criteria have equal weight:

1. Technological implementation: a working, non-trivial project built thoroughly and skillfully with Codex.
2. Design: a complete and coherent product experience rather than an isolated technical experiment.
3. Potential impact: a credible solution to a specific problem for a real audience.
4. Quality of the idea: a creative concept that differs meaningfully from existing alternatives.

## Guided demonstration

Demo Mode provides isolated time controls for reaching transaction deadlines without waiting. These controls never affect another session or protected builder accounts.

Demo Mode also provides isolated buyer and seller location controls so judges can demonstrate Handover Confirmation without moving two physical devices. Location remains a supporting signal rather than proof of a meeting.

### Successful handover

Show fixed-price purchase, simulated Escrow, seller-centric scheduling, a location-supported handover, buyer and seller Handover Confirmations, and simulated seller payout.

### Contested Material Mismatch

Show the buyer describing the mismatch, the seller disputing it and keeping the item, both parties leaving the Handover Point, and a simulated Jualokal review refunding the buyer.

No-show, cancellation, and Scheduling Expiry may appear as prepared example states but are not part of the main walkthrough.

## Demo listing media

- Prefer original photographs of physical items owned by a team member or used with the owner's permission.
- Provide at least three views per item, including one complete-item view.
- Label the associated account and listing as demo data; Demo Mode never offers the physical item for a real sale.
- Use plain backgrounds without faces, home details, addresses, reflections, or other identifying information.
- Remove embedded location and device metadata before adding an image to the repository.
- Prefer unbranded items or hide visible third-party logos, especially in the public demo video.
- Use original synthetic images only as a clearly labelled fallback when a suitable item cannot be photographed.
